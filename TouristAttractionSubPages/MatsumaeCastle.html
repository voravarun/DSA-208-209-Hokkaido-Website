Matsumae Castle
Hokkaido

Description

Matsumae Castle is a castle located in Matsumae in Hokkaidō, Japan, and is the northernmost castle in Japan. The only traditional style Edo period castle in Hokkaidō, it was the chief residence of the han of the Matsumae clan of feudal lords from the 17th to 19th centuries.In 1854, the castle was expanded and renamed Matsumae Castle. It had three towers, 15 gates, and a 25-cannon armory. Walking through the grounds of Matsumae Castle feels like stepping into another world. The scent of salt water and seasonal flowers mingle in the air.With around 10,000 trees and 250 different varieties of cherry blossoms, Matsumae Park is an amazing place in spring. Thanks to the many varieties of cherry blossoms in the park, Matsumae has a longer-than-usual blooming season, with the delicate pink and white blossoms appearing from late April through late May. During this time, Matsumae Castle hosts a cherry blossom festival complete with delicious festival foods.


Some of the special things to see:


Yokozuna Museum
Yokozuna Museum is dedicated to two local former sumo wrestlers, Chiyonoyama and Chiyonofuji. Chiyonoyama was a powerful wrestler in postwar Japan, who quickly rose up to the highest rank of yokozuna. His protege, Chiyonofuji, was one of the lightest yokozuna in modern times, yet also one of the most powerful and successful.

Cherry Bloosom
Over ten thousand cherry trees representing more than 250 different varieties can be seen around the grounds, and have earned the park the distinction of being ranked among the top 100 cherry blossom spots in Japan.

Transportation and Cost

Matsumae is located near the southwestern tip of Hokkaido about 100 kilometers west of Hakodate. A train line used to run to Matsumae, but it was discontinued in 1988.

From Hakodate Station

There are three direct buses per day between Hakodate Station and Matsumae (3 hours, 2100 yen one way, not covered by the Japan Rail Pass).

From Kikonai Station

These days, Matsumae is reached by bus from Kikonai Station (95 minutes, 1300 yen one way, one bus every 1-2 hours, not covered by the Japan Rail Pass). Kikonai is served by the JR Hokkaido Shinkansen (covered by the Japan Rail Pass) and by the South Hokkaido Railway (1 hour, 1170 yen one way from Hakodate, not covered by the Japan Rail Pass).
